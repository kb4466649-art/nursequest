Fundamentals of Nursing · NORCET
Q1. The most appropriate site for administering an intramuscular injection in a healthy adult, when a large volume of medication is required, is:
The ventrogluteal site is preferred for adults because it is free of major nerves and blood vessels, has a thick muscle mass, and can safely accommodate larger volumes (up to 3 mL) compared to the deltoid.
Medical-Surgical Nursing · RRB
Q2. A patient with congestive heart failure is prescribed furosemide. Which electrolyte imbalance should the nurse monitor for as a priority?
Furosemide is a loop diuretic that promotes potassium loss along with sodium and water, making hypokalemia the priority monitoring concern, as it can precipitate dangerous cardiac arrhythmias.
Obstetric Nursing · AIIMS
Q3. The normal fetal heart rate range in a term pregnancy is:
A normal baseline fetal heart rate is 110–160 beats per minute. Rates outside this range may indicate fetal distress and require further assessment.
Pediatric Nursing · CHO
Q4. As per the Universal Immunization Programme, the BCG vaccine is administered:
Under India's Universal Immunization Programme, BCG is given as a single dose at birth (or as early as possible up to one year of age if missed) to protect against severe forms of tuberculosis.
Mental Health Nursing · Staff Nurse
Q5. Which of the following is a therapeutic communication technique used by nurses in psychiatric settings?
Active listening and reflecting the patient's feelings back to them validates their experience and encourages further expression, which is a core therapeutic communication technique.
